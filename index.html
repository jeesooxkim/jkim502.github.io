MOBILE DEVELOPER

Speech Banana is an exercise-based application designed to aid deaf individuals who have received cochlear implants. These individuals have lingering impairment in communication due to underdeveloped listening skills. Through easily accessible systemized listening exercises, we aim to improve their communication abilities, and consequently, their quality of life.

I am the developer of the Android version of the app, and I am also in charge of maintaining the iOS version. In the future we plan to implement gaming components to the chapter exercises to create an engaging user interface, in order to encourage younger users. We are also seeking to increase our reach within the deaf community by working on a British English version and a Korean version of the application. We are currently undergoing preparations for clinical trials.

CHECK IT OUT : http://speechbanana.jhu.edu/

By providing an easily accessible, inexpensive, and non-invasive treatment option to Parkinson's patients that is easily performed at home, we hope to revolutionize motor symptom treatment of Parkinson's Disease.

RESEARCHER

Cahan Lab focuses on accurate and efficient analysis of RNA-seq data through developing a cloud-based platform (written in R language) called CellNet in order to improve the accuracy of stem cell differentiation with respect to their target cells. This platform utilizes AWS.

My current project focuses on human RNA-seq data collection of biopsied samples from mass online databases, such as GEO accession data and GTX data, and improving the efficiency of the R- and Python-based CellNet pipeline in order for quicker processing and analysis of existing human RNA-seq accessions. I debugged the program and made changes so that human RNA-seq data is also able to be processed. I am currently feeding collected data through analysis algorithms, and obtaining standard data which will eventually serve as a baseline for accuracy comparison for ex vivo differentiated cells.

CHECK IT OUT : www.cahanlab.org

DATA ANALYST

This lab focuses on analysis of cell FRET scans in order to better understand the effect of receptor mutations on cancer development/tumor metastasis, as well as investigating potential treatments by exposing the receptors to specific ligands.

I worked as a data analyst, running thousands of cell FRET and donor scans through a MATLAB program in order to determine fluorescence differences depending on different mutations, ligands, and dilutions. I am also currently in process of co-authoring a paper.
