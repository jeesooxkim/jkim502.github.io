Center for Bioengineering and Innovation

TremTex, an undergraduate biomedical engineering project team, focuses on building an in-home tDCS treatment device for reducing Parkinson’s Disease patients’ tremors. By providing an easily accessible, inexpensive, and non-invasive treatment option to Parkinson's patients that is easily performed at home, we hope to revolutionize motor symptom treatment of Parkinson's Disease.

We went through extensive prototyping, including CAD and 3D printing, and iterations of business plans for our device. U was particulraly involved in developing prototypes for the device, and also performed all presentations to faculty and experts, including a presentation in JHU's 2016 Business Plan Competition, in which our team placed Third Place in the Undergraduate Medical Technologies Track.
